Three Prolific Composers, Three Different Eras

Johann Sebastian Bach lived from 1685-1750. Bach is one of the most prolific baroque era composers. He is most well known for his work with 4 part harmonies, fugues, and counterpoint. Bach wrote countless pieces most of which were revolutionary for his time and are regarded as masterpieces.

Baroque The Baroque era in music lasted from approximately 1600-1750. Baroque music has a few key features such as long flowing melodic lines, littered with ornimentation, trills, and turns. Additionally baroque music played with contrasting dynamics LOUD and soft often pitting these opposites against eachother to get the most from their juxtaposition. One of the most notible things in this era was the use of contrapuntal textures which saw more than one melodic line occur at the same time. All of these features wrapped up in time appropriate instrumentation and an emphasis on musician's abilities rather than compositional ingenuity.
